Who benefits from the [[llm-knowledge-base-concept]] and how. Three primary personas identified by [[god-of-prompt]], plus notable real-world adopters.

## Primary Use Cases

### Creators and Marketers — Content Research Engine

Dump competitor breakdowns, trending articles, and audience insights into `raw/`. The wiki surfaces patterns and content angles you'd never find manually. [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

### Founders and Consultants — Second Brain for Client Work

Market research, competitive analysis, client domain knowledge. Every report generated feeds back into the system. By month 3, the AI knows your domain better than most hires. [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

### Students and Researchers — Deep Research

What [[andrej-karpathy]] actually built it for. Deep research across dozens of papers, with the AI tracking how ideas connect, where authors disagree, and what gaps remain. [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

### Business R&D Workflows

Also applicable to business research and development workflows (flagged as secondary use case by God of Prompt). [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

## Notable Adopters

**Andrej Karpathy** — deep research; ~100 articles, ~400K words on a single topic. Originated the pattern. [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

**Lex Fridman** — runs a similar setup. Generates interactive HTML visualizations and creates "mini-knowledge-bases" loaded into voice mode for 7–10 mile runs. [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

**Elvis Saravia (DAIR.AI)** — building LLM knowledge bases for AI research curation. [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

## Adoption Signal

Multiple open-source implementations appeared on GitHub within 48 hours of Karpathy's gist. [[god-of-prompt]] characterizes this as "becoming standard practice for anyone doing serious research." [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]
