AI researcher and educator, formerly Director of AI at Tesla and founding member of OpenAI. Known for making complex ML concepts accessible. Originated the [[llm-knowledge-base-concept]] pattern in April 2026.

## LLM Knowledge Base Contribution

On April 2, 2026, Karpathy posted about using LLMs to build personal knowledge bases for research topics. He dropped a full GitHub Gist that reached 5,000+ stars and 1,400+ forks within two days. [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

His own implementation reached ~100 articles and ~400,000 words on a single research topic — written, linked, categorized, and maintained entirely by the AI. [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

He described his approach as "a hacky collection of scripts" and acknowledged room for a real product to emerge from the pattern. [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

## Key Quotes

> "The human's job is to curate sources, direct the analysis, ask good questions, and think about what it all means. The LLM's job is everything else." [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

On filing explorations back into the KB:
> His own explorations and queries "always add up" in the knowledge base. [Source: Post by God of Prompt (@godofprompt).md]

## Notable Practices

- Processes sources one at a time (supervised), not in batch
- Uses the Obsidian Web Clipper for capturing articles as markdown
- Applies [[knowledge-compounding]] by filing query outputs back into the wiki
- Recommends keeping each wiki focused on one domain; separate KBs for separate domains
